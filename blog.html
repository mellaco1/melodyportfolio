TV

Within the last year, I have developed an increased interest in the art of TV. I love the idea that you can view entertainment live. I feel that there might even be more of a connection between the creators and the audience-- everyone is watching at the same time. There is also such a craft to the way each episode is created; I know I am constantly impatiently waiting for the next episode of multiple shows!

I have had the privilege to work on two TV projects so far. Last year, I directed the pilot episode of an “Office" spinoff, called "Public Education," and am so happy that I got to go through that experience. It felt so special, being a part of something like “The Office,” because the show means a lot to me. When I began to watch it a few years ago, it captivated me and took me on a temporary vacation away from reality. I wanted the same for this new show. Throughout the creation of this pilot, I learned so much about the millions of parts that coincide in the television industry and gained a lot of hands-on experience in the leadership position that is directing.

This year, I created an original TV show with some of my peers. I volunteered to direct the pilot episode, and jumped at the opportunity to help some of my classmates with the writing process. With a bunch of collaboration and patience, we were able to successfully finish the episode, and show it to the school. And, I learned a new skill: how to edit with Adobe Premiere!



Some other interests that have made me who I am today are: Public Relations, American Sign Language, volunteering, and organizing.
