Breaking Open My Shell

My shell broke a few years ago, after stepping foot in my second home. I used to be painfully shy, but being surrounded by people who think, act and create art like me pushed my limits in a positive way. I don't gloat about myself, but because of this school I am much more confident in who I am, how I create my art, and my flaws. Much has changed since GHAA, but most of the changes are things I would never reverse.

It's Not Just a TV Show

One of my friends introduced me to the hit tv show "The Office" last year. I watched that show day and night, and enjoyed every single minute of it. I can't say that I relate to any of the characters in any way, but the ingenius plot and elements were enough for me to fall in love with everything about it. The show, as many people would agree, is hilarious. It distracts you from the stressors in life and takes you to a paper company far, far away. I am instantly captivated and swept away in laughter.

"The Office" helped me become more outgoing. I've latched on to the character Dwight and can't stop myself from quoting him. I won't stop myself! This past Halloween, I dressed up as Dwight at my home school. It was a huge step for me, since at that school I was always a shy girl who minded her own business and focused deeply on school work. I still can't believe that I was Dwight at that school. When I went to GHAA that day, I made so many people smile that I would do it again in an instant. The moral of the story: get obsessed with a tv show and spread your obsession with the world!
