Who Am I?

My name is Melody and I am a student and artist. Ever since I was little, I have been creative, always thinking of movie plots and stories to produce. I had the privilege to attend the Greater Hartford Academy of the Arts for all of high school, where I got the chance to further explore types of writing and media. Before attending GHAA, I only knew how to write fiction, and edit short films on iMovie. But I have since learned how to lead a group of peers on a film set, edit with both Final Cut Pro and Adobe Premiere, write poetry, code a website (this website!), create a logo, and so much more.

My greatest success thus far was a four year project (that I'm still working on) by the name of "The Keys to Phobia" (TKTP). It was a novel at first, but after a year of writing I decided to turn it into a movie. I filmed it first in the summer of 2016, then ran out of time and decided to start over the year after. I spent the following school year revising the script and became more organized. In the summer of 2017, I tried again. This time, I was able to successfully film the whole movie, and even better, I was proud of it. I edited with Final Cut Pro and premiered the movie on August 11, 2018, at a Cinema in Madison, Connecticut. It was the best day of my life. I still dream to finish the novel, and hope to one day accomplish that dream.

I long to work with a media production company one day, especially one that focuses on documentaries. My main passion is video editing, but I am also interested in learning about and being a part of the other proccesses. I am looking forward to just learning more about some other companies, and hearing what they do!

My goal is to dip my feet into many different forms of Communication, while collaborating with others, creating meaningful work, and becoming a well-rounded person.
