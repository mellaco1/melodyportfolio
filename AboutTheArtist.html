Who Am I?

My name is Melody and I am a student in high school. Art has been a part of my life for as long as I can remember. Since I was little, I would always be thinking of movie plots and stories to write in my journals. Never did I think that growing up would challenge my art in unexpected ways, let alone expand it. I've had the privilege to attend an arts school for all of high school, where I get the chance to work on my writing and media skills. I have found myself the happiest when I am at this school.

I used to be shy. I would keep to myself every feeling because a part of me thought that being emotional was too vulnerable, and being too vulnerable wasn't good. But as I matured, I learned that everyone is vulnerable at some point, and art is such a good way to express feelings. I made that true.

My greatest success thus far was a four year project (that I'm still working on) by the name of "The Keys to Phobia" (TKTP)." It was a novel at first, but after a year of writing I was convinced to turn it into a movie. I filmed it first in the summer of 2016, then ran out of time and decided to start over the year after. I took the schoolyear to revise the script and become better organized. In the summer of 2017, I tried again. This time, I was able to successfully film the whole movie, and even better, I was proud of it. I edited with Final Cut Pro and showed the movie on August 11, 2018, at a Cinema in Madison, Connecticut. It was the best day of my life.

I long to be on a professional film set one day, but dream aside, have other interests: I'd like to work with people in the field of Public Relations and do film on the side, look into Media Relations, or become a film editor for local companies.
