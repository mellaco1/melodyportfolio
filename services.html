FILM

Through high school and college, and also on my own, I have made a countless amount of videos. But I have a particular few that I feel best represent who I am and what my artistic style is.

I used to gravitate towards fiction, but as I have grown and learned more have explored other mediums, such as documentary and TV. Now, my primary interest is working with documentaries. I love telling people's stories in the form of a video. My main passion is the video editing aspect, but I also love devising the interview questions and being present for the person's responses.

My most recent work is displayed for you first, and my oldest work is at the bottom.



Rusty Documentary

In my recent freetime, I created a documentary surrounding my dog, Rusty. He has had a pretty unique life-- one that I want to share with the world. It took hours and hours to find our home video footage, select the useable clips, interview my family, and edit the video. I am very proud of how it turned out.



Curry College Counseling Center Video

In one of my college classes, we were tasked with creating a video that advertised one of the departments the school has to offer. My group chose the Counseling Center. We interviewed a handful of students and faculty, and then I edited the footage into the video before you.



Competition: Corona, Episodes 3 and 7

During the Corona pandemic, I wanted to do something that would put my creative energy to use. My sister and I decided to make a fun competition series, called "Competition: Corona." Using our phones, we filmed each episode, and then I took a day to edit and post them. We know how hard this time is for everyone, and are doing this to entertain ourselves, and others. Smiles and laughs are the goal!

Our two favorite episodes are the ones listed here, Episodes 3 and 7. Enjoy!



GHAA Class of 2020 Senior Video

In my senior year of high school, I decided to create a video that would act as a keepsake for my peers. It turned into much larger of a project than I had originally intended it to when COVID-19 became a global issue, and cancelled schools. I had just filmed the staff and teacher interviews and intended to film the student interviews the next week. I struggled to cope with the reality that faced me, but ultimately decided to continue to work on this project.

I reached out to as many of my peers as possible and received many submissions. I spent a full day editing this project together after finding out about the "too close for comfort" deadline. I'm proud of this final product, and am so thrilled that I stuck with it. It made me realize how much I like filming and editing these types of projects. Non-fiction. Real. Raw. Collaborative. Meaningful.
